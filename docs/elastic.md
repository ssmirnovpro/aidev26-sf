# Elastic (Elasticsearch Labs)

## About the Company

**Elastic** builds Elasticsearch, one of the world's most widely used search and analytics engines. The company trades publicly on the NYSE under ticker ESTC. Elasticsearch Labs is their developer-focused division that creates blogs, notebooks, and educational content for the engineering community.

## AI Dev 26 Participation

**Role:** Sponsor.

Elastic was a sponsor of AI Dev 26. **Elasticsearch Labs** is their platform for developers building with GenAI, embedding models, and semantic reranking—positioning Elasticsearch as infrastructure for advanced search in the AI era.

## Product

**Elasticsearch Labs** is a unified developer platform for learning about and building advanced search solutions with Elasticsearch, covering everything from GenAI and embedding models to reranking and beyond.

### Key Features (from materials)

- Blogs and notebooks from Elastic's technical teams
- One-stop destination for developers working on advanced search
- Full support for GenAI, embedding models, and reranking capabilities

### Platform Technical Capabilities

- **Embedding models**: Support for Jina AI (jina-embeddings-v3), Elastic's built-in models, plus integrations with Google Vertex AI and IBM watsonx
- **Semantic reranking**: Elastic's own Rerank model (cross-encoder), plus Jina Reranker and IBM watsonx Rerank integrations
- **Hybrid search**: Combines keyword and vector search with semantic reranking
- **Open Inference API**: Unified API for connecting different embedding and reranking models
- **Vector database**: Native integration with Google Cloud Vertex AI as an information retrieval engine

### Integrations

- Google Cloud (Vertex AI, AI Studio)
- IBM watsonx
- Jina AI
- AWS

## Business Model

**Model: Subscription + Open Source (Open Core)**

- **Elastic Cloud Hosted** — subscription-based cloud service with Standard, Gold, Platinum, and Enterprise tiers (starting at $99/month)
- **Elastic Cloud Serverless** — usage-based pricing
- **Self-managed** — license-based on nodes and RAM, with Basic (free), Platinum, and Enterprise tiers
- **Pricing models**:
  - Hosted: resource-based (RAM/storage)
  - Serverless: usage-based
  - Self-managed: license-based on nodes and used RAM
- **Additional revenue**: Consulting, training, and certification

**Recent changes**: Elastic eliminated per-endpoint pricing for Elastic Security XDR, removing the "endpoint tax" to encourage broader security coverage.

### Pricing Notes

- **Cloud Hosted**: Entry tiers start at $99/month (Standard), with pricing based on allocated resources (RAM/storage/compute)
- **Cloud Serverless**: Usage-based billing
- **Self-managed**: License fees based on number of nodes and RAM, plus infrastructure costs
- Multi-year contracts come with discounts
- Elastic eliminated per-endpoint pricing for Security XDR in March 2026

Costs vary significantly based on deployment model, data volume, retention requirements, and feature usage.

## Analysis

Elastic is positioning itself as infrastructure for AI applications, extending its classic search capabilities into RAG pipelines and agentic AI. Elasticsearch Labs is becoming an educational hub for developers working at the intersection of search and GenAI.
