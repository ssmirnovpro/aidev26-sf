# AI21 Labs

## About the Company

**AI21 Labs** is an Israeli AI company focused on Natural Language Processing (NLP). Founded in Tel Aviv in November 2017 by three co-founders: Yoav Shoham, Ori Goshen, and Amnon Shashua.

## AI Dev 26 Participation

**Speaker:** Ori Goshen — Co-CEO & Co-Founder

**Talk Topic:** Building practical, enterprise-ready AI systems and reliable AI deployment at scale.

## Products

### Jamba — Foundation Model

Proprietary model built on a hybrid Mamba-Transformer architecture (also described as "Joint Attention and Mamba"):
- Context window up to **256,000 tokens**
- Optimized for long-context processing
- Low latency and fast inference
- Available models: **Jamba Large 1.7**, **Jamba Mini 2**, **Jamba 3B 2**
- Supports private enterprise deployment (VPC, on-premises)

**Use cases:** Summarization, legal document analysis, research tasks.

### Wordtune — B2C Product

AI-powered writing and reading assistant:
- Text refinement
- Content generation
- Document summarization
- Contextual understanding

### Enterprise AI Platform — B2B

Platform for enterprise AI deployment:
- Building reliable AI systems
- Integration with existing business processes
- Private deployment

## Business Model

**Model: API Platform (Pay-per-use) + B2C Subscription + Enterprise Contracts**

**B2B (Jamba API & Enterprise Platform):**
- Pay-per-token for API calls
- Enterprise licenses for private deployment
- Custom integration and support

**B2C (Wordtune):**
- Freemium — free version with limitations
- Premium subscription — expanded features

**Customers:** Boston Consulting Group, Bain & Company, Moderna, Amgen, Gates Foundation, JetBlue, Booking.com

**Revenue streams:**
- API consumption (Jamba)
- Wordtune subscriptions
- Enterprise contracts
- Private deployment licenses

## Analysis

AI21 Labs positions itself as an alternative to OpenAI and Anthropic for enterprise customers, with a strong focus on private deployment and long-context processing. The company's $1.4B valuation (2023) and backing from Intel Capital and Comcast Ventures validates their enterprise market position. Jamba's hybrid Mamba-Transformer architecture provides a technical edge in long-context scenarios with lower compute costs.
